FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is compound interest? ▼
Compound interest is interest earned on both your initial capital and the interest already accumulated. Each period, earnings are reinvested so that your money grows exponentially over time — like a snowball rolling downhill, picking up more mass as it goes.
What is the difference between the free and Premium version? ▼

Premium unlocks 10+ advanced KPIs (IRR, ROCE, Payback, Yield on Cost, NPV, Multiplier…), the Financial Freedom (FIRE) module with crossover point and milestones, and removes all ads. It is a one-time payment — no subscription.
What is CAGR? ▼
CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) is the annualised rate at which an investment would have grown from start to finish if it had grown at a steady rate every year. It is very useful for comparing investments with different time horizons on a like-for-like basis.
How does the Financial Freedom calculation work? ▼
snowballFi uses the 4% rule (FIRE method): the capital needed for financial independence is 25× your annual expenses. The app calculates when your projected capital will reach that target, the crossover point (when annual interest exceeds your contributions) and intermediate milestones at 25%, 50%, 75% and 100% of the target capital.
Do the calculations use the nominal or effective rate? ▼
snowballFi uses the nominal rate divided by the number of periods (standard banking convention, equivalent to Excel FV and the HP 12C). So a 7% annual rate with monthly compounding uses a periodic rate of 0.5833% per period — which is what users expect and matches standard financial calculators.
